Translation



Vigatarāga spoke:

|19.20| The gods are of eight kinds, animals are of five kinds. Mankind is said to be only one single [kind]. How come that there is the system of four social classes (varṇa)?

Anarthayajña spoke:

|19.21| It [i.e. the system of four social classes] was created by Lord Viṣṇu in the previous aeon[s]. Before the very beginning of all aeons, there was a single class (varṇa) of Brahmins (dvija).

|19.22| The Brahmins (vipra) got to know all the Vedas and all the sacrifices. These thousands of Brahmins (vipra) developed an inclination to make a resolution to perform sacrifices.

|19.23| Having understood the intention of the thousands of senior Brahmins (vipra), the Brahmins (brāhmaṇa) commenced performing rituals (karman) and the tasks (karman) were distributed.

|19.24| Some took on the function of being priests (r̥tvij), some took on the task of protection. Some got engaged in the acquisition of materials and others were appointed to do manual crafts.

|19.25| This is how they started performing sacrifices in the beginning. With the tasks (karman) thus distributed, the will to perform sacrifices increased.

|19.26| Then came all the R̥ṣis and all the gods and the Ancestors. They discussed it among themselves, the divine R̥ṣis, the Ancestors and the gods.

|19.27| Class arose for the sake of sacrifice. .... Proceed in this very manner, Sirs, O excellent twice-born!

|19.28| Those Brahmins (brāhmaṇa) who are now engaged in sacrifice and recitation, those good Brahmins (suvipra) shall become Brahmins (vipratāṁ yāntu), always engaged in the six duties [of Brahmins] (ṣaṭkarma).

|19.29| As for those Brahmins (vipra) that have been appointed to protect [the sacrifice] with weapons in their hands, to function as the protectors of the Brāhmins (vipra), they shall follow[?] the vow of Kṣatras.

|19.30| As for those twice-born who have been appointed for the acquisition of materials, they shall become Vaiśyas, involved in the means of business???

|19.31| Those of the twice-born who have been appointed to the tasks of slaughering and tying [animals] and of manual skills, they all shall become Śūdras.

|19.32| The Prājāpatya rank belongs to the Brahmins (brāhmaṇa) because they are devoted to the sacrifice and to recitation. The Aindra rank belongs to the Kṣatriyas because they are devoted to the protection of the people.

|19.33| The Vāsava rank belongs to the Vaiśyas who earn their living by trade and agriculture. The rank of Marut belongs to the Śūdras who devote themselves to sevice.

|19.34| Understanding the intention of the great R̥ṣis, the Ancestors and the gods, lotus-born Brahmā, the Grandfather, decided firmly.
