Translation

Chapter Nineteen

Cows

Vigatarāga spoke:

|19.1| By what action can the great Dharma, whose rituals are subtle[?], be reached? I ask for an easy method for mankind, tell me about it.

Anarthayajña spoke:

|19.2| I shall teach you the great Dharma that is the easy method, O Brahmin. Listen to that action by which heaven can be reached easily.

|19.3| Cows are the mothers of the worlds. Cows hold all the world. All cow-dung is nectar, all are produced by Śiva’s will.

|19.4| Cows contain all the gods. The Brahmin contains all the gods. Earth contains all the gods. Śiva contains all the gods.

|19.5| Therefore cows are always to be served because they give religious duties, liberation, financial gain and success. They should be provided with food, shelter, water, etc., with all one’s effort.

|19.6| One should not beat them too hard...? Protect them in thick [darkness or when in multitude], in case something is broken or they are startled.

|19.7| In case of pain from disease or wound, one should apply remedy using medicine. Rubbing should be done as much as it is pleasurable for cows.

|19.8| By circumabulating cows with faith and devotion, the whole Earth up to the oceans gets circulambulated.

|19.9| If a man touches a cow with faith ...... his sins, be them committed at daylight or at night, will disappeat, no doubt.

|19.10| He who applies the water that has been dispersed by a [cow’s] tail onto his head, will have his sins accumulated throughout his life destroyed, no doubt.

|19.11| One should bathe the cows as prescribed, using water onto which mantras have been recited. If he himself bathes in the same water, he will have all his sins destroyed.

|19.12| Diseases, obstructing forces, and bad luck will disappear instantly. Those with dead offspring or without offspring praise this very bath.

|19.13| If a man collects the ‘horn-water’ of cows and applies it on his head, he will receive the fruits of bathing at all the sacred pilgrimage places.

|19.14| If somebody gives a handful of food to cows with devotion, by this an Agnihotra is being performed and all the gods become satisfied.

|19.15| He who collects on his head the four streams [of milk] from the teats will receive the meritous fruits of visiting the four oceans.

|19.16| He who gives his life for cows during an attempt at stealing them, O greatest of Brahmins will prosper in Śivaloka for millions of years.

|19.17| If a man rears all [the cows] that have missing or broken [limbs] CHECK, will get all the fruits that are said to be produced by donating millions of wives,

|19.18| and will go to Śivaloka. When descended from Śivaloka, he will become a universal monarch on Earth.

|19.19| I have taught [about cows] truly, in brief, O supreme Brahmin. It is impossible to talk about the excellence of cows in more detail.

Vigatarāga spoke:

|19.20| The gods are of eight kinds, animals are of five kinds. Mankind is said to be only one single [kind]. How come that there is the system of four social classes (varṇa)?

Anarthayajña spoke:

|19.21| It [i.e. the system of four social classes] was created by Lord Viṣṇu in the previous æon[s]. Before the very beginning of all æons, there was a single class (varṇa) of Brahmins (dvija).

|19.22| The Brahmins (vipra) got to know all the Vedas and all the sacrifices. These thousands of Brahmins (vipra) developed an inclination to make a resolution to perform sacrifices.

|19.23| Having understood the intention of the thousands of senior Brahmins (vipra), the Brahmins (brāhmaṇa) commenced performing rituals (karman) and the tasks (karman) were distributed.

|19.24| Some took on the function of being priests (ṛtvij), some took on the task of protection. Some got engaged in the acquisition of materials and others were appointed to do manual crafts.

|19.25| This is how they started performing sacrifices in the beginning. With the tasks (karman) thus distributed, the will to perform sacrifices increased.

|19.26| Then came all the Ṛṣis and all the gods and the Ancestors. They discussed it among themselves, the divine Ṛṣis, the Ancestors and the gods.

|19.27| Brahmā/Viṣṇu (vidhi) created class for the sake of sacrifice. [The classes are] for the purpose of rituals (kratu). Proceed in this very manner, Sirs, O excellent twice-born!

|19.28| Those Brahmins (brāhmaṇa) who are now engaged in sacrifice and recitation, those good Brahmins (suvipra) shall become Brahmins (vipratāṃ yāntu), always engaged in the six duties [of Brahmins] (ṣaṭkarman).

|19.29| As for those Brahmins (vipra) that have been appointed to protect [the sacrifice] with weapons in their hands, to protect the Brāhmins (vipra) from injury, they shall eternally follow[?] the vow of Kṣatras.

|19.30| As for those twice-born who have been appointed for the acquisition of materials, they shall become Vaiśyas, involved in the means of trade.

|19.31| Those of the twice-born who have been appointed to the tasks of slaughering and tying [animals] and of manual skills, they all shall become Śūdras.

|19.32| The [world] of Prajāpati belongs to the Brahmins (brāhmaṇa) [after death] because they are devoted to the sacrifice and to recitation. The [world] of Indra belongs to the Kṣatriyas because they are devoted to the protection of the people.

|19.33| The [world] of Vāsus belongs to the Vaiśyas who earn their living by trade and agriculture. The [world] of Marut belongs to the Śūdras who devote themselves to sevice.

|19.34| Understanding the intention of the great Ṛṣis, the Ancestors and the gods, lotus-born Brahmā, the Grandfather, it [i.e. the system of varṇas] was established firmly.

|19.35| All the main domestic animals, birds and wild animals that are born in the world,

|19.36| CHECK .... [The social classes] have been taught briefly. What else do you wish to hear?
